## §5 — Lane B2: xiPLN — A Correct and Complete Foundation for PLN

**Source:** `ai-agents/lean-projects/mettapedia/papers/xiPLN.tex` (102 KB / 1,922 lines)
**Authors:** Zar (Zarathustra Goertzel) and Codex CLI (OpenAI GPT-5.2) — DRAFT
**Frame:** This is the architectural *manifesto* paper — the one that names the cluster's central pivot.

- C-B2-1. **The xiPLN slogan**: *"Probability is what you query; evidence is what you carry."* (Verbatim, paper L142-144.) This is the architectural core. The cluster's central distinction is between (i) the evidence carrier `(n+, n-)` that PLN passes around, and (ii) probability views (strength, confidence, intervals) that are projections from evidence. **Conflating these layers is the cluster's historical error**, per the paper.

- C-B2-2. **Three-layer separation** (paper §2 / L101-144): Layer 1 = world-model posterior states (complete semantics); Layer 2 = evidence algebra (quantale/Heyting structure); Layer 3 = operational views (strength, weight, confidence, bounds). xiPLN's claim: *all three layers exist and must not be conflated*. Earlier PLN presentations conflated them, leading to "semantic drift" where algebraic operators were treated as if they were the logic itself.

- C-B2-3. **Layer 1 reference instance is `JointEvidence(n) = Fin(2^n) → ℝ≥0^∞`** — Dirichlet pseudo-counts over the 2^n complete worlds for n propositional atoms. Revision is pointwise addition. (Paper L111-117.) This is exponential in n; it is the *complete* layer, not a tractable layer.

- C-B2-4. **Layer 2 is the carrier `(n+, n-)`** — the data structure that propagates through PLN inference. With `hplus` (revision = coordinate-wise add), order, lattice structure, Heyting operators. This is what `BinaryEvidence` in the Lean tree implements, and what `lib_wmpln_evidence.metta` mirrors in CeTTa.

- C-B2-5. **Layer 3 are projections.** Strength, weight, confidence, ITV intervals are *views* — information-losing projections from evidence. The paper explicitly notes Walley-style predictive IDM intervals and Bayesian Beta-posterior credible intervals are *distinct semantics* and "must remain explicit selector choices rather than being conflated into one operator" (paper L137-139).

- C-B2-6. **The No-Go Theorem (paper §5 / L320-345):** *"There is no function f: Evidence^5 → Evidence that, for all joint evidence states E, computes the exact link evidence for A⇒C from only the local premises Evidence(A), Evidence(B), Evidence(C), Evidence(A⇒B), Evidence(B⇒C)."* Lean proof at `Mettapedia/Logic/PLNJointEvidenceNoGo.lean` constructs two joint states agreeing on premises but disagreeing on `linkEvidence(A,C)`. **This is the cluster's central theorem** — the mathematical justification for replacing local-rule chaining with world-model posterior-state calculus.

- C-B2-7. **The Markov Screening No-Go (paper §6 / L346-388):** PLN's additive deduction is *structurally wrong* for Markov composition. The screening condition `proj(A→C, rowA + rowB) = proj(A→C, rowA)` requires `rowB` to contribute zero at coordinate C — false in general. The honest alternative: predictive chaining via `markovWMPosteriorChain` and `markov-transition*` atom family. **Cross-validation with PeTTaChainer**: the paper notes PeTTaChainer's `MpFormula` uses strength multiplication s_AB · s_BC, which is *consistent with* this finding (paper L386-387). This is the formal validation of the multiplicative MP heuristic that Source 5 traced to leungmanhin's 2023 PR.

- C-B2-8. **Classic PLN rules become compiled tactics or admissible query rewrites under explicit Σ side conditions** (paper L263-318, §3.4). The chain BN exactness theorem `chainBN_plnDeductionStrength_exact` is cited at `PLNBayesNetFastRules.lean:924` (per Codex C-B2-6, verified). Standard PLN deduction strength formula computes the correct conditional probability P(C|A) — *under explicit positivity side conditions*. Without those Σ conditions, PLN deduction is heuristic, not exact.

- C-B2-9. **The Factor Graphs distinction (paper §8 / L432-489):** semantic factor graphs (world-model factorizations, exact via VE/JT) vs operational factor graphs (inference control, heuristic scheduler). Alignment requires *compilation* from a world-model class. **This is the strongest theoretical correction to Source 9 Lane 1's FactorGraph papers**: the FactorGraph-on-MORK proposals were operational (control); the xiPLN view is that they're not automatically semantic.

- C-B2-10. **Roadmap explicitly marks operational closure as Chapter 9 future work** (paper L516-570): "Chapter 9 future work (operational closure): complete the operational large-scale inference layer once the Lean-side MeTTa runtime stack is stable enough for executable chainer/runtime integration; until then, Ch. 9 remains theorem-level and counterexample-first on the Lean side." **This is the explicit author statement that runtime/.metta closure is pending** — the formal answer to Source 10's open question about lib_pln_xi.metta. The paper itself acknowledges the runtime gap.

- C-B2-11. **B2 source-of-truth check (HEADLINE):** xiPLN.tex names ZERO `.metta` files. Independent grep on `lib_pln_xi`, `lib_wmpln`, `lib_pln.metta`, `wmpln_evidence`, and `\.metta` returns no matches. **Gemini's Source 11 V-11-3 claim is unsupported by the source.** Codex C-B2-10 is correct: the runtime equivalence between `CeTTa/lib/lib_wmpln.metta` and the cited `lib_pln_xi.metta` is not established by the paper. The paper references `Mettapedia/Logic/*.lean` artifacts and "PeTTa," but no concrete `.metta` filename. **Cluster narrative**: lib_wmpln.metta and lib_pln_xi.metta share the evidence-layer algebra (Layer 2) but the JointEvidence + Σ-guarded query-plan layers are NOT realized in lib_wmpln.metta — they are theorem-level only.

## §6 — Lane B3: World-Model Calculus for PLN

**Source:** `ai-agents/lean-projects/mettapedia/papers/world-model-calculus.tex` (56 KB / 1,295 lines)
**Authors:** Zar and Oruži — DRAFT
**Frame:** The systematic theory paper, written with broader scope than xiPLN. Subtitle: *"Evidence, Views, and Σ-Guarded Compiled Inference."*

- C-B3-1. **Architectural thesis (paper L65-99):** PLN inference is **stateful posterior revision plus evidence extraction**. "Fast PLN rules" are **compiled, Σ-guarded rewrites** whose soundness is discharged relative to a declared world-model class (Bayesian network, factor graph, joint evidence, etc.). This is xiPLN expressed as a fully-articulated calculus, not just a foundational draft.

- C-B3-2. **The semantic spine (paper L172-176):** the essential law for any WorldModel typeclass is **additivity of evidence extraction under revision**: `ev(W1 ⊕ W2, q) = ev(W1, q) ⊕ ev(W2, q)`. This is the algebraic constraint that makes PLN a *calculus* rather than a heuristic rewrite system. For the concrete `PathMap`/`Finset α` instance, this law is Lean-proven as `pjoin_evidence_additive` in `OSLF/PathMap/PLNBridge.lean`. The paper proves: K&S sum rule, monotonicity, Bayesian conditioning partition, STV connection (`Strength = |W ∩ q| / |W|`). Standard Lean kernel axioms only. (Paper L180-189.)

- C-B3-3. **The Multiset variant resolves an idempotency obstacle (paper L191-195):** Finset union is idempotent (`W ∪ W = W`), which breaks `evidence_add` for non-disjoint pairs. Multiset sum is the free commutative monoid, enabling proper non-idempotent revision. **This is mathematically essential** — without the Multiset variant, the WM calculus only works for disjoint stores. (Codex caught this at C-B3-3.)

- C-B3-4. **Three judgment variants (paper L216-222):** `WMJudgment W` (context-free), `WMJudgmentCtx Γ W` (set-indexed, tightens trust boundary), `WMJudgmentMulti Γ W` (multiset-indexed, tracks multiplicities — records if same observation used twice). The provenance discipline is built into the type system; double-use detection is structural, not heuristic.

- C-B3-5. **Σ-guarded rewrite rule definition (paper L237-247):** A rewrite rule = (side condition Σ, conclusion query q, derived evidence term `derive(W)`, proof that `Σ ⇒ ∀W. derive(W) = ev(W, q)`). **Many PLN rules rewrite only the strength view** — they prove an identity about Strength(ev(W, q)), not full evidence. This is the formal mechanism by which historical PLN rules can be re-validated as compiled tactics under explicit assumptions.

- C-B3-6. **Three layers of query equivalence (paper L252+):** all formalized. Wiki should treat this as one of the cluster's most rigorous formal artifacts.

- C-B3-7. **The "pass distributions, not just truth values" mantra (paper L574-577):** the closing position is that the complete layer is *not* a competing "second PLN" — it is **the reference semantics that operational PLN rules approximate, under explicit assumptions, within declared model classes**. This frames the cluster's two-paradigm reality: production PLN is *operational PLN*, World-Model PLN is *reference semantics*. Both can coexist if the operational rules are explicitly Σ-guarded.

- C-B3-8. **The status-note discipline (paper L97-101):** *"Lean-proven claims are those tied to explicit Lean file/theorem references (primarily §Lean Artifact Map). Other sections are research synthesis, design rationale, or roadmap. Verify against current build outputs before citing as formalization state."* This is the same hygiene as the MORK-PathMap paper's gap inventory. Both papers explicitly mark the boundary between formal claim and research narrative. Wiki should adopt similar discipline when citing these papers.

**Code-implementation status:** PROTOTYPE / FORMALIZED-THEORY. Lean infrastructure is real but is theorem-level, not production runtime.

## §7 — Lane B4: Markov-de Finetti Formalization

**Source:** `ai-agents/lean-projects/mettapedia/papers/markov-definetti-formalization.tex` (62 KB / 1,455 lines)
**Authors:** Mettapedia Project — April 2026
**Stats per paper L54-67:** **26,665 lines of Lean code, 0 sorry, 33 files**, building on a 43,683-line classical (i.i.d.) de Finetti formalization. (Lean 4 + Mathlib.)

- C-B4-1. **The Diaconis-Freedman 1980 theorem formalized:** every Markov-exchangeable, recurrent prefix measure on a finite alphabet is a mixture of time-homogeneous Markov chains (paper L99-110). For Markov sequences. The classical (i.i.d.) de Finetti is exchangeability ⇒ mixture of i.i.d. laws; Markov de Finetti is the same idea for Markov-exchangeability + recurrence.

- C-B4-2. **Why this matters for PLN:** under exchangeability, **binary counts `(n+, n-)` are sufficient statistics** for exchangeable binary sequences; transition-count matrices are sufficient for Markov-exchangeable sequences (paper L608-637). **This is the deepest mathematical justification** in the cluster for "evidence is what you carry." The carrier `(n+, n-)` is not arbitrary — it's the right object under specific exchangeability assumptions, and those assumptions are what xiPLN's Σ side-conditions formalize.

- C-B4-3. **Recurrence is not optional.** The paper gives an explicit counterexample (deterministic 0→1→1→… chain) that is Markov-exchangeable but non-recurrent and *fails* mixture representation (paper L216-238). This is methodologically important: the cluster's PLN math implicitly assumes recurrence; failing that, the carrier sufficiency claim fails.

- C-B4-4. **The Solomonoff connection (paper L580-607):** structured universal mixtures. Markov-de Finetti justifies a mixture over Markov parameters, with hyperpriors over Dirichlet rows giving computable dominance-style guarantees. **This connects PLN evidence to Solomonoff induction at a foundational level** — the cluster's evidence carrier is a finite-alphabet specialization of universal Bayesian inference.

## §9 — Cross-paper synthesis: the architectural narrative

This is the Claude-emphasis lane (per brief). The four Lane B papers form a coherent *theoretical architecture* with explicit dependencies:

```
                    Markov-de Finetti (B4)
                     [Why (n+,n-) is the right carrier]
                              │
                              ▼
                       xiPLN (B2)
              [Three-layer separation; No-Go theorem;
                Σ-guarded compiled tactics]
                              │
                ┌─────────────┴────────────┐
                ▼                           ▼
        World-Model Calculus (B3)   MORK-PathMap (B1)
        [Systematic formal calculus] [Substrate formalization
         [Proof-theoretic skeleton]   with PLN evidence bridge]
```

- **B4 grounds the cluster's data structure.** The carrier `(n+, n-)` is foundationally justified — it's not a heuristic choice. It's a sufficient statistic under exchangeability.
- **B2 is the manifesto** that names the three-layer architecture and proves the No-Go theorem that makes the architecture *necessary*.
- **B3 systematizes B2** as a Σ-guarded calculus with precise judgment forms and rewrite-rule discipline.
- **B1 builds the substrate** that B3's PathMap/Finset/Multiset instances run on.

Together, these four papers describe a **cluster-internal World-Model PLN paradigm** that exists in formal Lean form but has not yet been productionized.

### The two-paradigm cluster reality

The PLN cluster now has **two paradigms** that coexist:

**Paradigm 1: Local-Rule PLN (production)**
- Where: `PLN/`, `PLN3` (trueagi-io), historical OpenCog PLN
- What: confidence-priority forward-chaining over a fixed local rule set
- Truth values: STV/EV with ad-hoc multiplicative confidence (pln0.9 pivot, leungmanhin 0.2 hack)
- Status: **formally known to be incomplete** (B2 No-Go theorem)
- Pragma: ships, runs, scales — but provably cannot compute exact link semantics for arbitrary world models

**Paradigm 2: World-Model PLN (Lean-formal)**
- Where: `ai-agents/lean-projects/mettapedia/Mettapedia/Logic/`, `CeTTa/lib/lib_wmpln*.metta`
- What: posterior-state revision + evidence extraction with Σ-guarded compiled rewrites
- Truth values: evidence carrier + projections + explicit views (Walley vs Bayesian as separate selectors)
- Status: **theorem-level** with substantial Lean infrastructure; runtime closure pending per author statement (xiPLN §6)
- Pragma: rigorous foundations, sufficient-statistic justification, No-Go theorem, but **not yet a production runtime**

The cluster's central question — *which paradigm wins?* — is unresolved. Paper-side, World-Model PLN is the obvious successor. Code-side, Local-Rule PLN is the only thing that runs today.

### Five-tradition language (Codex's proposal, refined)

Codex proposed five traditions for the cluster narrative; this synthesis adopts and refines:

1. **Production Local-Rule PLN line** — `PLN/`, `PLN3`. Operational PLN as historically practiced.
2. **Historical OpenCog/URE/ECAN control** — URE Bayesian Thompson + MixtureModel (Source 6); ECAN attention-currency (Lane A1). **Largely abandoned in the MeTTa migration** (Sources 6-7). Worth citing as the historical control architectures the cluster *had* but lost.
3. **Adjacent ai-agents Python+Lean** — atps/scripts (premise selection) + Mettapedia Lean (formal proofs). The benchmark + formalization workspace.
4. **CeTTa active runtime** — `lib_pln.metta` (production-style), `lib_wmpln*` (World-Model variant), biomedical regression tests. *Active runtime line*, not production deployment.
5. **MORK substrate + formalization** — Rust MORK as substrate (Source 8 confirmed); ai-agents/mettapedia MORK-PathMap formalization paper as adjacent Lean spec (B1).

Wiki cluster map should adopt this five-tradition language to avoid the trap of presenting the cluster as a single coherent codebase.

### Methodology note for wiki citations

Both B1 and B3 papers explicitly mark the boundary between Lean-proven claims and research-narrative claims. B4 documents that even "zero sorry" can hide assumptions encoded as Props. **The wiki should adopt the same discipline:** when citing a Lean theorem from these papers, distinguish:

- *"Lean-formalized"* (with file:line)
- *"Theorem-level / no production code"*
- *"Research synthesis / design rationale"*

This is more nuanced than the Source 9 vocabulary (IMPLEMENTED / PROTOTYPE / PAPER-ONLY) and is needed because the World-Model line is genuinely novel — formal artifacts at production-Lean scale that have no production runtime equivalent.
