## Goal

This is a **migration audit**, not a standalone repo summary. The headline question from Source 6's reconciliation:

**Did URE's Bayesian inference-control architecture (Thompson Sampling, BetaDistribution, MixtureModel, ControlPolicy, BIT) survive the migration to `chaining`, or was it lost?**

Source 6's reconciliation §6 noted this is the single highest-priority cluster-level open question. **Resolution determines whether the cluster's "no inference control in MeTTa-era PLN" gap is:**
- (a) **migration + simplification** (the architecture lives, just simpler / different language),
- (b) **partial migration** (some pieces, not all),
- (c) **conceptual loss** (the architecture died with URE).

Gemini's preliminary discovery confirmed `thompson-sample` exists in `experimental/pln-inf-ctl/pln-inf-ctl.metta`. **But "thompson-sample exists as a function" is not the same as "the full BIT+ControlPolicy+MixtureModel+BetaDistribution+meta-control-rule stack survived."** The agents must produce a precise feature-by-feature comparison.
