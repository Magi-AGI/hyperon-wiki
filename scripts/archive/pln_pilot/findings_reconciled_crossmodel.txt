## 1. Disputes resolved by cross-model source verification

These are the four formula/content disputes Claude's internal triangulation surfaced. All are now resolved by Codex (and confirmed by Gemini where noted).

### 1.1 [RESOLVED] Consistency-condition lower bound — denominator is `sA`
**Resolution (Codex C-2-2, Gemini verification):** The Maple-form consistency condition in Ch. 5 §5.2.2.2 ~p. 82 uses:
`max((sA+sB-1)/sA, 0) ≤ sAB ≤ min(1, sB/sA)`

Claude agents 1 and 2 were correct (denominator `sA`); Claude agent 3 was a transcription error (`sB`).

### 1.2 [RESOLVED] Symmetric Modus Ponens — multiplication form
**Resolution (Codex C-2-8, Gemini verification):** Ch. 5 §5.7.2 ~p. 112 algebraic reduction:
`sB = sA · simAB + c · (1 − sA) · (1 + simAB)`

Claude agents 2 and 3 were correct (multiplication); Claude agent 1 division by `(1+simAB)` was a derivation error.

### 1.3 [RESOLVED] Inverse-approximation coefficients — two sub-tables, both real
**Resolution (Codex C-1-8, Gemini verification):** Ch. 4 §4.5.1.2 ~p. 61-62 presents **two coefficient tables**:
- **Full k-dependent table:** `A_{111} = -0.00875486, …, B_{222} = -0.071902027` (Claude agents 2 and 3)
- **Simplified k-independent approximation:** `A11 = -2.922447948, …, B22 = -0.107482534` (Claude agent 1)

Not a contradiction. The simplified form is for general use; the k-indexed form is for high-precision implementations. Wiki citations should say which table a coefficient comes from.

### 1.4 [RESOLVED — genuine source bug] Biconditional rendering p. 210
**Resolution (Codex C-2-15, Gemini verification):** The source-text page 210 contains **both**:
- `(P ⇔ Q) ≡ (¬P ∨ Q)` — this is *implication* (`P ⇒ Q`), not biconditional
- `(P ⇔ Q) ≡ (¬P ∨ ¬Q)` — this is *NAND*, classically wrong

And separately the correct form: `(P ⇔ Q) ≡ (P ⇒ Q) ∧ (Q ⇒ P)`.

Gemini: "A confirmed OCR/Source Bug." Codex: "should be treated as a source-text problem rather than a valid transformation rule." Both rendering errors are present in the extracted text — likely OCR damage of `↔` / `⇒` glyphs, though the possibility of a genuine typographical error in the original 2008 book is not ruled out.

**Wiki implication:** never cite or reproduce these two erroneous equivalences. Cite only the implication-pair form.

## 2. Unanimous cross-model findings (all three models independently surfaced)

### 2.1 [X3] URE and ECAN are not mentioned in the 2008 PLN book

All three models confirm. The book's integrative architecture is exclusively the Novamente Cognition Engine (NCE). Attention is handled via NCE "importance" values (short/long-term). Any wiki card attributing URE or ECAN machinery to the PLN book cites post-2008 Hyperon-era work, not this book.

### 2.2 [X3] Modus Ponens is the authors' own "very bad" heuristic
`sB = sAB·sA + c·(1 − sA)`, `c ≈ 0.02`. Gemini: "simple but highly error-prone heuristic." Codex: "very bad / highly error-prone, but still better than the obvious trivial alternatives." Only used as fallback when term-logic deduction can't apply.

### 2.3 [X3] M2I / I2M are badly wrong for heterogeneous sets
Cohesion is a prerequisite. All three models cite the book's "Things owned by Izabela" failure case (Ch. 5 §5.8 ~p. 113).

### 2.4 [X3] Combinatorial explosion in inference control is explicitly unsolved
Book's own admission: pruning heuristics only work for "fairly simple inferences." Integration with pattern mining, evolutionary learning, attention allocation is *required* (not optional) for general use.

### 2.5 [X3] Constant-extraction from ∃ violates classical logic under indefinite TVs
`∃x: G ∧ F(x) ≠ G ∧ ∃x F(x)` in general. Book defers resolution to a "future paper" (Ch. 11 §11.3). Concrete counterexample given.

### 2.6 [X3] Causal inference requires Novamente importance values
Distal cause vs. enabling condition distinction is not a pure-PLN phenomenon. Gemini: "chapter will merely scratch the surface." Codex confirms importance/attention language lives at Novamente level only. All three: mentions of ECAN-style attention are from post-2008 re-attribution.

### 2.7 [X3] Induction can't be globally rescued by any universe-size choice
Ch. 8 §8.1. System must abandon a single global "space of events." Gemini: confirmed. Codex C-5-5: "forces abandonment of a single global space of events."

### 2.8 [X3] Chapter 8 error-magnification produces chaotic trajectories
Gradient analysis `|∇f| < 1` required for error to attenuate. Without careful inference control, long probabilistic chains replace meaningful signal with magnified random error. Gemini: "chaotic trajectories." Codex: "effectively consume the available precision bits." Claude (A3 only internally, now cross-validated): 10 steps at magnification 2 erases 10 bits of precision.

### 2.9 [X3] Frame problem is side-stepped, not solved
Probabilistic persistence + contextual default reasoning, not crisp circumscription. All three models confirm.

### 2.10 [X3] PLN is "evolving," not final
Book's explicit positioning. Authors anticipate further refinement or replacement of components.

## 3. Cross-model consensus on core technical content

Organized by section. These claims are in Claude's reconciled doc and corroborated by Codex/Gemini.

### 3.1 Truth-value semantics

- **SimpleTV `<s, w>`** and dual **count form `<s, N>`** with `w = N/(N+k)` — [X3].
- **IndefiniteTV `<[L,U], b, k>`** — default representation in current practice; semantics "probability `b` that after `k` more observations the ordinary probability lies in `[L,U]`" — [X3].
- `b = 1` → Walley-style imprecise probabilities; `b < 1` is what keeps intervals narrow under chaining — [X3].
- **DistributionalTV**: under discretization, deduction = matrix multiplication, inversion = matrix inversion — [X3].
- **Walley interval form** `[m/(n+k), (m+k)/(n+k)]` — [X3].
- **Beta-binomial conversion theorem** with uniform prior — [X3].
- **Inverse-function approximation** `n = -ln(b/A)/B` with two coefficient sub-tables — [X3, with §1.3 resolution].
- **No formal error-analysis** for the inverse coefficients — acknowledged book gap — [X3].
- **Context macro**: `Context C R A B <t> ≡ R (A∧C) (B∧C) <t>` — [X3].
- **Universe size** smaller → more speculative; larger → less error; no extra computational cost to large `|U|` — [X3].
- **Quantifiers use third-order probabilities** — envelope of envelopes — [X3].

### 3.2 Rule taxonomy (first-order core)

- **Deduction** (independence): `sAC = sAB·sBC + (1 − sAB)·(sC − sB·sBC)/(1 − sB)` — [X3]. Provably biased for cohesive sets (underestimates).
- **Inversion / Bayes**: `sAB = sBA·sA/sB` — [X3].
- **Induction** (= Inversion ∘ Deduction): `sAC = sBA·sBC·sB/sA + (1 − sBA·sB/sA)·(sC − sB·sBC)/(1 − sB)` — [X3].
- **Abduction**: `sAC = sAB·sCB·sC/sB + sC·(1 − sAB)·(1 − sCB)/(1 − sB)` — [X3].
- **Concept-geometry deduction** (cohesive sets): 1-D form `sAC = sAB·sBC / min(1, sAB + sBC)`; corrects independence underestimation — [X3].
- **Consistency conditions** enforced via Heaviside multipliers; deduction returns 0 outside — [X3, resolved §1.1].
- **Term-probability inference**: `sB = sA·sAB/sBA` (Bayes rearrangement) — [X3].

### 3.3 Rule taxonomy (conversions, Booleans, HOI)

- **M2I / I2M**: preserve strength, decrease confidence; cohesion required — [X3].
- **Revision** (simple): `s = w1·s1 + w2·s2`, `n = n1 + n2 − c·min(n1, n2)` — [X3].
- **Revision c-parameter drift**: with `c > 0`, iterated revision drifts strengths to 1; no general tuning algorithm — [X3].
- **Distributional revision** with indefinite TVs: mix second-order distributions, re-extract quadruple — [X3].
- **Boolean extensional operators** `ANDExt`, `ORExt`, `MINUSExt`; **intensional** `ANDInt`, `ORInt`; **mixed** `ANDMix`, `ORMix` — [X3] (less depth in Gemini).
- **AND, OR, NOT** probability formulas (independence assumption for AND) — [X3].
- **Higher-order Boolean transforms** on p. 210 — [X3, with §1.4 audit flag].
- **SatisfyingSet operator** reduces HOI to FOI — [X2: Claude, Codex; Gemini implicit].
- **Variable Instantiation** rule (cohesion required) — [X2: Claude, Codex].
- **Combinators** (B, C, I, S) for variable-free forms — [X2: Claude, Codex].

### 3.4 Rule taxonomy (quantifiers, intensional, temporal)

- **Quantifier rules** (Existential Gen, Universal Spec, Quantifier Exchange) hold under indefinite TVs — [X3].
- **Constant-extraction from ∃ fails** — [X3, see §2.5].
- **Fuzzy quantifiers** via sliding `[LPC, UPC]` (AlmostAll = [0.9, 0.99], AFew = [0.05, 0.1]) — [X2: Claude, Codex].
- **Intensional deduction** = convert to extensional, run ordinary deduction, convert back — [X3].
- **Intensional inheritance** via pattern-sets — [X3].
- **Pattern-intensity** `IN(F,G) = [c(G) − c(F)]+ · ASSOC(F,G)` — [X2: Claude, Codex].
- **Temporal relationships**: SeqAND, DisjointSeqAND, EventualSeqAND, SimAND, SimOR — [X3 with varying depth].
- **PredictiveImplication**: `Implication P(S) [SeqAND(P,Q)<T>](S)` — [X3].
- **EventualPredictiveImplication** (no interval): `starve → die` type — [X3].
- **PredictiveChain** — [X2: Claude, Codex].
- **Causal filter** via Intensional PredictiveImplication (rooster/dawn example) — [X2: Claude, Codex].

### 3.5 Rule taxonomy (large-scale, Ch. 9)

- **Multideduction** (batch rule with inclusion-exclusion factor ε) — [X2: Claude-A1/A2, Codex].
- **Batch Reasoning Graph** (BRGI): DAG optimization, quality `c·N + (1-c)·T/N` — [X3].

### 3.6 Inference control

- **Forward / backward chaining** with probabilistic twists — [X3].
- **BIT (Backward Inference Tree)** evolutionary interpretation: genotype = atoms+rules, fitness = weight-of-evidence — [X3].

- **Inference trails** (bounded length, ~10–20 entries) — [X3].
- **Rule of Choice** 4-option fallback — [X2: Claude, Codex]; Gemini mentions Rule of Choice without detail.
- **Default inference at control level, not rule level** — [X3]. Penguin/bird worked examples: Claude/Codex.
- **Termination** on weight-of-evidence threshold, not first-path — [X2: Claude, Codex].

## 6. Pilot workflow assessment (for cross-model triangulation)

**Intra-Claude triangulation (3 sub-agents)** produced a rich detail catalog and surfaced 4 formula disputes that would not have been visible from a single pass. Duration ~40 min/agent in parallel.

**Cross-model triangulation (Claude + Codex + Gemini)** resolved all 4 disputes by source verification and added model-unique framings (Codex's engineering framing, Gemini's historical framing). The cross-model step:
- confirmed all X3 unanimous findings with high confidence;
- identified Claude-only claims worth re-verifying;
- exposed a genuine source-text bug (p. 210 biconditional).

**Cost vs. value:** the combined ~2–3 hours of wall clock and ~6× the tokens of a single Claude pass produced an extraction substantially more trustworthy than any single model could have. Recommended pattern for all subsequent high-stakes sources. For lower-stakes sources (small auxiliary repos, peripheral publications), single-model passes should suffice.
