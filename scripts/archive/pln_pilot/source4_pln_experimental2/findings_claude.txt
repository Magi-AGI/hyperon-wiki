### Motivation & programme

- **C-2-1** (nuPLN.tex §Introduction, L55-79) **νPLN's programme: approximate a first-order (unknown, computable) distribution μ using a second-order (uncomputable, known) universal distribution ν**, analogous to Solomonoff Induction. Verbatim: *"The goal is similar to Solomonoff Universal Induction, that is we want to approach a first order (unknown but computable) distribution μ given observations, using a second order (uncomputable but known) distribution ν, called the Universal Distribution."*
- **C-2-2** (L64-76) Key departure from Solomonoff: observations are *outcomes from an indexed boolean random variable representing the outputs of evaluating a predicate*. The predicate ("observable predicate") is *"a program drawn from a certain Probabilistic Programming Language"*. Without loss of generality, PLN's multiple observable predicates can be collapsed to one μ by adding a predicate-selector component to the domain.
- **C-2-3** (L77-79) *"Because this reformulation of PLN departs somewhat from the definition of PLN in the PLN book, we give it a new name, νPLN."* — explicit distancing from the 2008 book.
- **C-2-4** (L82-92) **This is the big theoretical shift vs. the PLN book**, verbatim: *"In its original form, PLN purposely avoids relying on an underlying global probability distribution. I am not against this in principle. I will simply admit that I cannot conceive a complete definition of PLN that does not rely on such global probability distribution. I would also point out that a publication released after the PLN book by the principal authors of the PLN book, Ben Goertzel and Matt Ikle, very much aligns with the idea of a global probability distribution, and was in fact a great source of inspiration for writing this very document."* The referenced Goertzel–Ikle publication is cited as `\cite{TODO}` — unidentified in the current draft (follow-up lead: it is `Goertzel2014EGI1/2/2Chapt18` or `Warrell2022` in `local.bib`, but nuPLN.tex does not actually resolve the `\cite{TODO}`).

### Formal machinery (§sec:globalprob, L94-220)

- **C-2-5** (L96-116) **Universe**: probability space `(Ω, F, ν)` where Ω is the sample space of possible worlds, F the σ-algebra event space, ν the universal distribution. Each world `ω̂ ∈ Ω` contains **(i)** a probabilistic predicate μ̂ ∈ L over domain D in a probabilistic programming language L, **(ii)** a complete mapping of μ̂ from D to Bool. The mapping is potentially infinite; an observer sees only a finite subset.
- **C-2-6** (L117-124) Concept of **"true world"** ω and **"true generator/predicate"** μ. Non-determinism: μ is probabilistic so the history is *one possible history among an infinity of histories compatible with μ*.
- **C-2-7** (L130-136) Probabilistic predicate type signature (verbatim): `μ : D → Ω → Bool` in curried fashion. *"Meaning that given its argument, it produces a boolean random variable. Therefore the observable predicate can be viewed as an indexed boolean random variable."* Multi-component domains curry uniformly: `μ : D_1 → … → D_n → Ω → Bool`.
- **C-2-8** (L149-167) Application notation `(μ x)` / `(μ x_1 … x_n)`; parametric form `μ_{x_1}` treating x_1 as parameter. Uses traditional math notation for non-μ functions (e.g., `ν(E)`).
- **C-2-9** (L168-193) **Two key random variables** access Ω:
    - `M : Ω → L` with measurable space `(L, F_L)`. Takes a world to the program generating it. *"Not accessible from an observer within that world."* Critical for higher-order reasoning.
    - `D_{x ∈ D} : Ω → Bool`. Boolean RV indexed by domain elements. Partially accessible: observer can gather data for finite subset S ⊂ D, giving a family `D_S`.
    - Equality: `(μ̂ x ω̂) = (D_x ω̂)` where `M(ω̂) = μ̂`; curried: `(μ̂ x) = D_x`.
- **C-2-10** (L194-220) **Bayesian inference in the universal framework** (verbatim formula):
  ```
  ν(M ∈ L | D_S) = ν(M ∈ L) × ν(D_S | M ∈ L) / ν(D_S)
  ```
  where `L ∈ F_L`, `S` is a finite subset of D. Shorthand dropping M: `ν(L | D_S) = ν(L) × ν(D_S | L) / ν(D_S)`. Author notes L must be continuous for the Bayesian approach to recover PLN (*"I will explain this in detail, but for now let us use this notation to formulate Bayes' theorem"*). **Prior over L is a parameter of νPLN**: *"given a certain prior of ν over L, one can derive a certain flavor of νPLN."*

### Relation to Solomonoff

- **C-2-11** (§PLN Global Distribution vs Solomonoff Induction, L222-232) *"unlike with Solomonoff Universal Induction, μ̂ represents a probabilistic predicate rather than a computable probability function calculating the probability of any event, although the latter can be derived from the former."*
- **C-2-12** (L228-232) Terminology: **"real predicate"** (objective reality) preferred over **"true predicate"** (which could be misread as always-outputs-true).

### Bridging book-PLN to νPLN (§3 PLN and the Global Distribution, L234-345)

- **C-2-13** (§3.1 Concepts vs Predicates, L241-252) **νPLN drops the concept side entirely**, keeping only the predicate side. Justification: *"there is a perfect isomorphism between concepts and inheritance on one side and predicates and implication on the other side"* (referencing PLN book §2.6 *Higher-Order Logical Relationships*). The book's parallel notation is collapsed for conciseness.
- **C-2-14** (§3.1.1, L254-293) **Major book-vs-νPLN reframing**: *"Originally, in the PLN book, the inheritance relationship is defined as an explicit mixture of extensional and intensional inheritances. We will show however that they are in fact both the same thing, the extensional inheritance is a way to approach inheritance solely via induction, and intensional inheritance is a way to approach inheritance solely via abduction."* — This is the νPLN dissolution of the ext/int split from Source 1 (V6 list: "intensional↔extensional conversion rules" marked absent).
- **C-2-15** (L266-286) Extensional inheritance = probabilitized subset relationship. Example: `(Inheritance Bird Fly) ≞ 0.9` = 90% of birds fly. Under sample uncertainty, a second-order distribution is used, and the book's second-order Beta distribution is adopted here. *"Under this assumption only two numbers are required to determine the parameters, α and β, of the associated Beta distribution."* `STV<s,c>` example: `(Inheritance Bird Fly) ≞ ⟨0.9, 0.99⟩` where 0.9 is strength, 0.99 is confidence. **Confidence encodes sample size: higher sample ⇒ higher confidence**.
- **C-2-16** (§3.1.2, L295-315) Isomorphism between concepts and predicates: concept→predicate via indicator function; predicate→concept via satisfying set. Same isomorphism applies to inheritance (concept side) ↔ implication (predicate side). Example: `(Implication IsBird DoesFly) ≞ ⟨0.9, 0.99⟩`.
- **C-2-17** (§3.2 Relating Predicates to μ, L317-334) **Novelty — how multiple predicates fold into single global μ**: add a subdomain `P` (symbols representing predicates) to μ's domain. Now `μ : P → D → Ω → Bool`. Example: *"a cat is a mammal"* becomes `μ_{Cat} ⟹ μ_{Mammal}`. Evaluation of *Cat* on instance `cat_123` is `(μ_{Cat} cat_123)` instead of `(Cat cat_123)`. — This is νPLN's answer to *"how does one global predicate handle the book's diverse concept/predicate lattice?"*
- **C-2-18** (§3.3 Truth Value as Posterior Distribution, L336-337) **Section is empty** — only labelled `sec:truthvalue`. Contents not written.
- **C-2-19** (§3.4 Statement versus Judgement, L339-345) Inherits NAL terminology: **statement** = logical statement sans TV (e.g., `P ⟹ Q`); **judgment** = statement + TV (e.g., `P ⟹ Q ≞ ⟨0.9, 0.8⟩`). *"Like in NAL"* — explicit Pei Wang / NARS citation, adding to Source 2's open NARS citation question.

  ν(L_B | D_S) = ν(L_B) × ν(D_S | L_B) / ν(D_S)       where L_B ∈ F_B

## §3 — Alternative truth-value types (Bl, Pr, STV)

**All Bl/Pr/STV type definitions and methods live in a single file:** `experimental/early-pln/common/truthvalue/TruthValue.metta` (143 lines). CLAUDE.md:57-60 documents them but the actual definitions follow.

### Verbatim type constructors

- **C-3-1** (TruthValue.metta:10) Base type: `(: TruthValue Type)`.
- **C-3-2** (TruthValue.metta:16-18) **Bl** — Boolean truth value, verbatim:
  ```
  ;; Boolean TV constructor
  ;; TODO: alternatively we could have (: ⊤ TruthValue) and (: ⊥ TruthValue)
  (: Bl (-> Bool TruthValue))
  ```
  Wraps a `Bool` into a `TruthValue`. Usage: `(Bl True)`, `(Bl False)`.
- **C-3-3** (TruthValue.metta:20-21) **Pr** — First-order probability truth value, verbatim:
  ```
  ;; First order probability TV constructor, i.e. mere probability.
  (: Pr (-> Number TruthValue))
  ```
  Wraps a `Number` (a probability in [0,1]) into a `TruthValue`. No confidence dimension.
- **C-3-4** (TruthValue.metta:23-26) **STV** — Simple truth value, verbatim:
  ```
  ;; Simple Truth Value.  A Second order probability TV constructor,
  ;; i.e. probability and confidence.  The probability is in fact the
  ;; mode of the corresponding beta distribution.
  (: STV (-> Number Number TruthValue))
  ```
  Two arguments: strength (mode of the associated Beta), confidence.

### Constants

- **C-3-5** (TruthValue.metta:32-45) Priors: `prior-alpha = 0.5`, `prior-beta = 0.5` — **Jeffreys prior** (explicit comment says so). `lookahead = 1.0` (count-to-confidence scale). `max-count = 1e9` (stand-in for +∞ until supported).

### Method dispatch over {Bl, Pr, STV}

- **C-3-6** (L86-91) `mode`:
  ```
  (= (mode (Bl True)) 1.0)
  (= (mode (Bl False)) 0.0)
  (= (mode (Pr $pr)) $pr)
  (= (mode (STV $pr $_)) $pr)
  ```
- **C-3-7** (L93-100) `count`: `Bl` and `Pr` both evaluate to `(max-count)` = 1e9; `STV` computes `(confidence->count $conf)`.
- **C-3-8** (L102-112) `confidence`: `Bl` → 1.0, `Pr` → 1.0, `STV` → its stored `$conf`.
- **C-3-9** (L114-120) `pos-count $tv = mode × count`; `neg-count $tv = (1-mode) × count`.
- **C-3-10** (L122-128) `post-alpha = prior-alpha + pos-count`; `post-beta = prior-beta + neg-count`. Uses the Beta-distribution Bayesian update.
- **C-3-11** (L135-142) `mean`:
  ```
  (= (mean (Bl True)) 1.0)
  (= (mean (Bl False)) 0.0)
  (= (mean (Pr $pr)) $pr)
  (= (mean (STV $pr $conf))
     (let* (($a (post-alpha (STV $pr $conf)))
            ($b (post-beta (STV $pr $conf))))
       (/ $a (+ $a $b))))
  ```
  Note `mean ≠ strength` for STV — it's the Beta-distribution mean after Jeffreys prior update.

### Interpretation vs. book / Sources 2-3

- **C-3-12** **Bl and Pr are NOT the IndefiniteTruthValue or DistributionalTruthValue the book describes.** The book's IndefiniteTV is a (L, U, b) triple representing a credible interval; the book's DistributionalTV is a full distribution. `Bl` is degenerate (count = 1e9, confidence = 1.0: a crisp Boolean assertion); `Pr` is a single-number first-order probability with no confidence dimension. **They are a down-step from STV, not an up-step** — strictly less expressive than STV. STV itself remains the single point of access to a second-order (Beta) distribution.
- **C-3-13** The TV hierarchy here is `Bl ⊂ Pr ⊂ STV` by expressiveness, all three unified into one `TruthValue` sum type. `Bl`/`Pr` are implemented as collapse cases for when confidence = 1 and either it's a Boolean or an arbitrary probability. Rule formulas like `Truth_Deduction`, `modus-ponens-formula`, `simple-deduction-strength-formula` only destructure `STV` (see `common/formula/ModusPonensFormula.metta:7-14`, `DeductionFormula.metta:39-54`); **no rule formula exists for Bl or Pr inputs**. They're defined but unused in inference.
- **C-3-14** The supplementary TV type `EvidentialTruthValue` (`ETV`) at `common/truthvalue/EvidentialTruthValue.metta:19` wraps `(OrderedSet $a) TruthValue` — it's an evidence-set-ascribed TV, used by `ImplicationDirectIntroductionEntail.metta` and `..._Match.metta`. And `TemporalTruthValue` (`TTV`, `TETV`) at `common/truthvalue/TemporalTruthValue.metta:22-26` wraps `(-> Number TruthValue)` and `(-> Number EvidentialTruthValue)` respectively — **this is the only "temporal" machinery in the repo**. Time is discrete (per comment). No rules consume `TTV`/`TETV` anywhere; just type declarations. (Unused scaffolding.)

### Verdict on §3 focus question

- **C-3-15** **Bl/Pr do NOT implement the missing IndefiniteTV or DistributionalTV hierarchy.** They're degenerate cases of STV (crisp-Bool and no-confidence-probability respectively). The book's TV hierarchy remains unimplemented in `pln-experimental2`.

## §5 — Confidence-formula continuity with pln0.9 pivot

`pln-experimental2` implements only 3 rules (Deduction, ModusPonens, ImplicationDirectIntroduction). Let me diff each confidence formula against Source 3's pln0.9 and Source 2's PLN3.

- **C-5-1 Deduction confidence** (`common/formula/DeductionFormula.metta:52`):
  ```
  (min $Pc (min $Qc (min $Rc (min $PQc $QRc))))
  ```
  That is `min($Pc, $Qc, $Rc, $PQc, $QRc)`. **This MATCHES PLN3** and **PREDATES** the pln0.9 pivot (which uses `$PQs · $QRs · $PQc · $QRc`, per Source 3 findings §2.2). 5-way min over all 5 premise confidences; no strength multiplication.
- **C-5-2 ModusPonens confidence** (`common/formula/ModusPonensFormula.metta:14`):
  ```
  (min (min $ABc $notABc) $Ac)
  ```
  where `$notABc = 1`. Effectively `min($ABc, $Ac)`. **Matches PLN3's `min($cAB, $cA)` pattern, PREDATES pln0.9's `$Ps · $PQs · $Pc · $PQc`.**
- **C-5-3 ModusPonens strength** (`ModusPonensFormula.metta:12-13`):
  ```
  (+ (* $ABs $As) (* $notABs (- 1 $As)))    ; where $notABs = 0.2
  ```
  Algebraically: `sB = sAB·sA + 0.2·(1-sA)`. **Identical to PLN3 asymmetric MP** (Source 2 verified `c=0.02` for MP; but here the comment at L10 calls 0.2 "temporary hack" and the `ModusPonensDTL.metta:21-27` docstring concurs: *"P(B|Not A) = 0.2 which is a temporary hack"*). **Constant is 0.2, NOT 0.02** — this is **different from PLN3/PLN's 0.02**. Worth flagging: the constant may be an order-of-magnitude typo or intentional experimental choice, but either way it diverges from the pln-cluster consensus of 0.02 and from the book's §5.7.1.
- **C-5-4 Deduction strength** (`DeductionFormula.metta:47-51`):
  ```
  (if (< 0.9999 $Qs)                  ; avoid division by 0
      $Rs                              ; Qs tends to 1 short-circuit
      (+ (* $PQs $QRs) (/ (* (- 1 $PQs) (- $Rs (* $Qs $QRs))) (- 1 $Qs))))
  ```
  **Matches PLN3's independence-based deduction formula verbatim.** Short-circuit threshold 0.9999 vs PLN3/PLN's 0.99 — a trivial numeric difference.
- **C-5-5 Consistency guard** (`DeductionFormula.metta:16-20`): same as PLN3: `$ABs ≤ min(1, $Bs/$As)` and `$ABs ≥ max(0, ($As+$Bs-1)/$As)` — denominator `$As`, in line with PLN3's `trueagi-io/hyperon-pln DeductionDTL.metta` provenance.
- **C-5-6 ImplicationDirectIntroduction formulas** (`common/formula/ImplicationDirectIntroductionFormula.metta:10-19`):
  ```
  direct-introduction-base-formula(qa) := STV(bool->number(qa), count->confidence(1))
  direct-introduction-recursive-formula(False, STV s c) := inc-neg-count(STV s c)
  direct-introduction-recursive-formula(True,  STV s c) := inc-pos-count(STV s c)
  ```
  **No PLN3/PLN counterpart** — this rule doesn't exist in Sources 2-3. So cannot compare. But the formulas themselves are book-legitimate: single-evidence case bootstraps with count=1 (confidence 0.5 per `count->confidence` with lookahead=1); recursive case does proper Bayesian updates.
- **C-5-7 Conclusion** — **`pln-experimental2` PREDATES the pln0.9 multiplicative-confidence pivot.** Its 3 rules use min-based confidence (Deduction, MP) or direct-Bayesian-update confidence (IDI), matching the PLN3 / Source-1-book pattern. There is no `Truth_or` usage. The repo's commits are 2026-03-12 ("Misc") per brief, so the code is recent but the formulas are from the pre-pivot era — the repo has simply not been updated to adopt the pivot, consistent with `experimental/README.md` flagging this code as "probably no longer relevant."

## §6 — Maxima symbolic derivations (`conjunction-xp.mac`, 387 lines)

**The `.mac` is an exploratory numerical/symbolic workbook studying the conjunction-introduction rule's second-order distribution under different independence assumptions.** It's computation-heavy (histograms, plots, fit-to-beta), not proof-theoretic. Key claims:

- **C-6-1 Primary question** (per README `truth-value-formula/conjunction-introduction/README.md:22-28`): *"What is the probability distribution obtained from applying a rule formula to premises which are themselves first order probabilities?"* i.e. for conjunction-introduction `A ≞ TVA, B ≞ TVB ⊢ (A ∧ B) ≞ TVAB`, what does TVAB look like once we integrate over possible first-order probabilities?
- **C-6-2 Core mathematical object** (`.mac:59-75`, README L72-98): the probability distribution of `|A ∩ B| = k` under uniform random placement, derived from binomial combinations and rewritten using Stirling's approximation:
  ```
  Pr(pAB = x) = (sqrt(pa·pb·(1-pa)·(1-pb)) / sqrt(2π·n·(pa-x)·(pb-x)·x·(x-pb-pa+1)))
              · ((pa^pa · pb^pb · (1-pa)^(1-pa) · (1-pb)^(1-pb))
                 / ((pa-x)^(pa-x) · (pb-x)^(pb-x) · x^x · (x-pb-pa+1)^(x-pb-pa+1)))^n
  ```
  This is defined in the `.mac` as `mstpr(n, pa, pb, x)` at L109, after 6 successive manual simplifications starting from a Stirling-substituted pcpr (L82) and culminating at the cleanest form.
- **C-6-3 First finding** (README L60-69): for independence sampling, as universe size `n → ∞`, the distrolet **converges to a Dirac delta at pAB = pA·pB**. Verbatim: *"considering all possible ways that A and B could be distributed without any additional knowledge about them other than the fact that they are uniformly randomly distributed is equivalent to assuming that they are independent!"* Numerically verified by plotting at n=50, 100, 200, 400, ... 4×10⁶.
- **C-6-4 Second investigation** (README L124-352): adds an extra premise `A → B ≞ TVab` as a dependency-capturing term. Derives the bounds `max(1+(pb-1)/pa, 0) ≤ pab ≤ min(1, pb/pa)` (assuming `0 < pa`). Uses "uniform distrolet" (uniform sampling within bounds) and "extreme distrolet" (bimodal at endpoints) as sampling strategies to compute the resulting TV on `A ∧ B`.
- **C-6-5 Third variation** (README L354-549): replaces `A → B` by `A ↔ B` (equivalence). Derives `pAB = (pa·pab + pb·pab) / (1+pab)` from `pab = P(A∧B|A∨B) = pAB/(pa+pb-pAB)`. Observed effect: over 9 premise-pair trials, mean of A∧B is greater under `A↔B` vs. `A→B` in 8/9 cases (statistically significant). Variance differences not significant.
- **C-6-6 Headline conclusion** (from README L348-352): *"the structure of a composite predicate, like a conjunction of two predicates, by itself provides some forms of evidence. In other words, evidence can be obtained not just from direct observations but from structures as well."* — theoretical finding that matters for νPLN.
- **C-6-7 Also in the `.mac`**: fit-alpha/fit-beta to fit an unscaled Beta to empirical mean/variance at L153-155; beta-distribution mean/variance formulas at L162-164; the `NEXT`-marked `fit_scaled_alpha`, `fit_scaled_beta`, `scaled_beta_distr_mean`, `scaled_beta_distr_variance` at L170-180 (incomplete implementations).
- **C-6-8 Relationship to implemented rules**: **The `.mac` work has NOT been ported back into any rule formula.** The existing `DeductionFormula.metta` and `ModusPonensFormula.metta` use the classical (pre-distrolet) formulas. There is no `ConjunctionIntroductionFormula.metta` at all in `common/formula/`. The Maxima investigation is **purely exploratory** and its conclusions have not influenced the code.
- **C-6-9 Relevance to pln0.9's multiplicative-confidence pivot**: the `.mac` work is **orthogonal** to the pivot. It does NOT validate or invalidate `$Ps · $PQs · $Pc · $PQc`; it studies something different — the full second-order distribution of the conclusion under different independence assumptions, not a closed-form confidence update. However, C-6-6 ("structure provides evidence") **arguably supports** the pivot's spirit: if structural relationships add evidential weight, then a confidence formula that's multiplicative in strength (and so picks up strength-induced evidence propagation) is more defensible than pure min-based aggregation.
- **C-6-10 `.mac` bugs / unfinished**: there are 2 typos in `stv_to_beta` (`nc: (1-strength)*c` uses undefined `c`; `prior_alpha: 1` re-binds rather than `prior_beta: 1`) at L14. Comment at L319-321 says *"NEXT: explore using A<->B instead of A->B"* — subsequently addressed at L319+ (done). 4 `NEXT` placeholders at L170-180 for scaled-beta fitting.
- **C-6-11 Discovered reference**: `PLN_AND_Confidence_Framework.md.pdf` — referenced from README L358 as "Ben's document". Not in local.bib. Suggests a **Goertzel private note** on the AND-confidence framework; may or may not be publicly available.
