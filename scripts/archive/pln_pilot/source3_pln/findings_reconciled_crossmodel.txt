### 2.2 Per-rule confidence formula deltas (verified from `lib_pln.metta`)

### 2.3 Behavioral implications

The shift to multiplicative-with-strengths confidence has concrete effects a domain expert will care about:

- **More conservative confidence propagation**: `$Ps · $PQs · $Pc · $PQc` is typically smaller than `$c1 · $c2`, so conclusions land with lower confidence.
- **Strength-sensitivity**: unlike book theory (which separates `s` and `c` through `Truth_w2c(min(...))`), PLN/'s new formulas entangle strength into confidence output.
- **`Truth_or` as an innovation**: `1 - (1-a)(1-b)` (probabilistic-or) appears in two rules for confidence. This isn't a book formula; it's a new heuristic.
- **Divergence from book theory**: the 2008 PLN book (Source 1) uses `min` or weighted-combination confidence aggregation. PLN3 kept the book's pattern. PLN/ has *deliberately* departed. Worth flagging as "PLN/ has moved further from the book than PLN3 did."

### 2.4 Why Claude and Codex missed it

Claude's error (C-2-5, C-2-6, C-2-7 of `findings_claude.txt`): cited the PLN/ formulas with new confidence expressions but labeled them "IDENTICAL" — apparent pattern-matching on the *strength* formula only, with the confidence line assumed-same.

Codex's error (C-2-2 of `findings_codex.txt`): claimed formulas are "present verbatim in the monolith" — confused *presence verification* with *unchangedness*. Did not line-by-line diff against PLN3.

Gemini's win: Gemini's output was concise and focused on "what changed?" framing rather than "which rules exist?" framing. The smaller output forced tighter comparison.

**Lesson for future reconciliation:** when models disagree on "identical vs changed," trust the one that quotes both versions and flags a difference over the ones that label something "identical" without an explicit diff.

## 4. New examples analysis (X3 consensus framing; Claude deepest)

### 4.1 `examples/DeductionRevision.metta`

Two-path deduction (A→B→D and A→C→D) followed by revision of disjoint-stamped conclusions. Expected TV `(stv 0.125 0.30429942952553224)` over stamps `(1 2 3 4)`. **Does NOT probe V8** (Gemini's hypothesis not borne out): stamps are disjoint, so `StampDisjoint` allows the merge and revision runs once — the monotonic-saturation pathology requires repeated revisions of overlapping-stamp evidence, which this test doesn't exercise.

### 4.2 `examples/Direct.metta`

User-space macro layer defining a custom `=>` operator + `?` query combinator. Compiles implication-like clauses into direct MeTTa reductions using `Truth_ModusPonens`, folds alternate derivations via `Truth_Revision`. **Bypasses the PQ-based Deriver entirely** — uses MeTTa's native reduction + revision aggregation. Not a new core rule family; a convenience front-end. Toy theory exercised: father/mother/grandfather/old.

### 4.3 `examples/RavenInduction.metta`

Induction over IntSet: given two individual ravens (rv1, rv2) with disagreeing black-membership evidence (0.8 and 0.0), induce `(Inheritance raven (IntSet black))` → `(stv 0.38 ...)`. **Inverts FlyingRaven's deduction direction** — good coverage complement. **Red flag [X1 Claude]**: uses `lib_patrick` import, diverging from the standard `lib_import`/`git-import!` flow documented elsewhere in the repo. Won't work under the standard PeTTa runtime.

### 4.4 Are these examples in the test sweep? [X2 Codex + Claude]

Yes — `test.sh` sweeps all `examples/*.metta` and `ruletests/*.metta`. The three new examples are part of the default regression run.

## 10. Source-5 retroactive correction — MP lineage genealogy

Source 5 reconciliation (`source5_hyperon_pln/findings_reconciled_crossmodel.txt`, 2026-04-24) revealed that this doc's framing of "`Truth_SymmetricModusPonens` uses constant 0.2 (semantically distinct from book's `c=0.02`)" was incomplete. The full lineage is:

- The 2023 `patham9/hyperon-pln` seed contains **exactly one** MP rule (`metta/common/formula/ModusPonensFormula.metta`), using `$notABs = 0.2` with `min`-based confidence. Authored by **Manhin Leung (leungmanhin)** in PR #23, drafted 2023-07-13 (commit *"Draft the Modus Ponens formula and DTL"*) with explicit *"temporary hack"* commentary added 2023-07-31.

- That seed rule **structurally became `Truth_SymmetricModusPonens`** downstream in PLN3 + PLN/, gated by `SymmetricModusPonensRuleGuard` for `Similarity` / `IntentionalSimilarity` / `ExtensionalSimilarity` link types. Its `0.2` constant is **inherited from leungmanhin's 2023 work**, not invented during pln0.9 or any later refactor.

- A **separate book-faithful asymmetric MP** (`Truth_ModusPonens` with `c = 0.02` per book §5.7.1) was **added downstream** in the production line (already present by PLN3 / Source 2; absent in the 2023 hyperon-pln seed). This is a NEW rule slotted alongside the inherited symmetric one — not a renaming or refactor of it.

**So the corrected reading of §1.2 above:** the `0.2` multiplication-form resolution stands; the formula is correct. But the "PLN3-specific datum" framing should be replaced with: **`0.2` is a 2023 `hyperon-pln` seed datum (Manhin Leung), inherited unchanged through PLN3 into PLN/**. The `0.02` `Truth_ModusPonens` rule is a separate, post-2023 book-faithful addition.

**Updated wiki implications** (supersedes earlier §7 entry on MP):
- When documenting PLN's Modus Ponens, distinguish two distinct rules by lineage:
  - `Truth_ModusPonens` (`c = 0.02`, book §5.7.1): book-faithful asymmetric, post-2023 addition.
  - `Truth_SymmetricModusPonens` (`$snotAB = 0.2`): leungmanhin's 2023 `hyperon-pln` seed, inherited verbatim into PLN3 and then PLN/.
- Cite PR #23 (commit `0d017c4` in `patham9/hyperon-pln`) when explaining the `0.2` constant's provenance.
- The pln0.9 confidence pivot (`Truth_or($sA, $sAB)` confidence formula change documented in §2.2 above) is a 2026 modification by Peter Isaev to the *symmetric* rule's confidence calculation only — the strength formula and `0.2` constant pre-date it by 3 years.
