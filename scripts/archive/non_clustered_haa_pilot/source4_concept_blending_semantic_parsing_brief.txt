§0. SOURCE IDENTITY
-------------------

This is a multi-topic source spanning two related-but-distinct research lines
that both touch the HAA cluster's "non-clustered residual" scope:

  S4.A — CONCEPT BLENDING. Goertzel's Hyperon-era formalizations of
         conceptual blending (Fauconnier & Turner-style integration
         networks) including information-theoretic, quantale-based, and
         paraconsistent-interference variants. Plus the iCog-Labs-Dev
         conceptBlending implementation repo with multiple methodological
         sub-projects.

  S4.B — SEMANTIC PARSING. The NL-to-Atomese / NL-to-MeTTa / NL-to-PLN
         pipeline lineage: Link Grammar (CMU; current maintained version),
         RelEx (Java; archival but recently maintained), opencog/learn
         (active neuro-symbolic language learning), opencog/generate
         (active syntax-network generation), and the rTreutlein/leungmanhin
         semantic_parsing + semantic-parser + nl2pln_demo pipeline. NOT
         including the bio-data-* / mindplex-* / mm-semantic-search
         enterprise variants which are application-specific and out of
         scope.

  S4.C — SGNN BOUNDARY PLACEMENT. Per HAA Source 3 V3-6 narrative-guard
         (closed 2026-05-06), glicerico/SGNN (Self-Governing Neural
         Networks for speech act classification on the SwDA corpus) was
         out-of-scope for Pattern Mining and deferred to S4 OR cross-org
         sweeps. S4 must finalize the placement decision.

These three sub-sources are bundled into a single brief because:
  - Both A and B are "non-clustered" residuals from the HAA cluster
    that don't deserve their own multi-source pilot.
  - Concept blending and semantic parsing share NL/cognitive-semantics
    infrastructure (Link Grammar in particular sits at the seam).
  - Reviewer extraction efficiency: a single brief reduces orchestrator
    + reviewer overhead vs two separate dispatches.

Reviewers should produce findings files with explicit per-sub-source
sections (S4.A / S4.B / S4.C) for clean reconciliation. Estimated
findings size ~250-400 lines per reviewer (between S2 and S3 in scale).
