## Mission frame -- what Source 4 must learn

Sources 1-3 closed with the following narrative:
- Classic OpenPsi (singnet @ `b31c7e3`) was Tradition 2 -- pragmatic engineered surrogates for the 2009 ECAN paper AND for the 2013 Cai et al. EAAI OpenPsi paper
- MeTTa OpenPsi port (`hyperon-openpsi` @ `3b356c5`) is a paper-leaning HYBRID reimplementation -- 8 LITERAL equations, 2 DIVERGENT (eqs 5 & 8 denominators), 1 ABSENT (eq 11 resolution), partial paper recovery but not strict-literal
- Glicerico fork -- divergent-but-stale extension with stimulus parameter
- Source 3 verdict: AVOID "Tradition 6" label without explicit qualification

**Source 4's job is to determine where the Hanson tradition fits in this framing.** Two competing hypotheses:

1. **Hypothesis H1 (Hanson-as-Tradition-2-extension):** Hanson's OpenPsi consumption was a runtime layer that consumed Tradition 2 surrogates faithfully -- via `opencog/openpsi/` Scheme bindings -- with no independent mathematical innovation. The Sophia production runtime executed OpenPsi rules but did not re-implement the math.

2. **Hypothesis H2 (Hanson-as-parallel-tradition):** Hanson sidestepped the OpenPsi math entirely by running affective state through Ghost dialogue rules, ros-behavior-scripting behavior trees, and SEC/feeling/emotion variables that bypassed the action-selector seam. The `(ghost-set-sti-weight 0)` finding in `loving-ai-ghost-leungmanhin/bin/run.scm:4` is the smoking gun: STI coupling was deliberately disabled in production. Therefore, the Hanson tradition is **functionally decoupled** from the literal-equations question.

The reviewers should produce evidence for or against each hypothesis, distinguish between them where possible, and surface any third pattern.

## Methodology rules accumulated through the cluster pilot

Reviewers must follow these rules from prior sources:

1. **Cross-source reconciliation rule**: Surface dissents explicitly with V4-N numbering. Do not paper over.
2. **Pickaxe before "never used"** (`feedback_pickaxe_for_dead_code.md`): `git log --all -S '<token>'`; current-HEAD grep is not sufficient. ECAN Source 3 V0-1 case: STI hook was wired 2016-2018, not "never".
3. **Both API layers** (`feedback_ecan_audit_token_list.md`): For any STI/AttentionBank coupling audit, grep BOTH C++-side AND Scheme-side. ECAN Source 4 V0-1 case: OpenPsi was missed via C++-only grep.
4. **Verify attribution against git** (`feedback_attribution_verify_against_git.md`): Cross-check attribution AND identity claims against the source. Gemini drifts on attribution and on V-N-X identity assertions.
5. **Caller-analysis before "actively coupled" claims** (Source 1 V1-1): Presence at the binding site is necessary but not sufficient. `rule-sca-weight` example: present in code, zero callers, therefore enabler-not-default.
6. **Bidirectional fork-divergence check** (Source 2 V2-1): "Forward divergence" (fork has feature X) and "behind upstream" can BOTH be true. Run `git log A..B` AND `git log B..A` AND grep both directions.
7. **Don't trust code comments as source attribution** (Source 3 brief rule #8): A comment that says "implements eq 12 from paper" is a hypothesis, not evidence. Verify against the paper independently.
8. **Equation-presence verification must be exhaustive** (Source 3 V3-1 implicit corollary): Selective verification of "prominent" equations creates false-positive Tradition assignments. If you don't verify all paper-relevant equations, do not assert paper-faithfulness.
