    `MeTTaIL/build:42` CLASSPATH="lib/antlr-4.9.3-complete.jar:

  The BNFC tool generates the parser/lexer from the `.cf`

  runtime — but the §7 vocabulary's `[BUILD-BNFC-FULL]` tag
  captures "BNFC is the full parser-generation path" not
  "BNFC executes at runtime". The runtime parser IS the
  BNFC-generated artifact; therefore the pipeline is BNFC-
  full from grammar through assembled-jar deployment.

  Per V2-PROTOCOL-1 specificity rule + Codex's stronger
  evidence chain, `[BUILD-BNFC-FULL]` is the right §7 tag.

    `FormalMeTTa/src/main/scala/RewriteRules.scala:136`
      State(i_, k, Space(t) ++ w, Space(??? : Term) ++ o)

  The `???` token in Scala is the standard placeholder for
  "method not yet implemented" — it compiles but throws
  NotImplementedError at runtime. Its presence in the
  AddAtom1Rule output path means FormalMeTTa cannot
  successfully execute add-atom operations — a substantive
  hole in the preliminary-spec implementation.

  Reconciling the two verdicts:
    - Gemini's `[FORMALMETTA-FAITHFUL-TO-2023-SPEC]` is
      contextually correct: FormalMeTTa IS pinned to the
      2023-era leithaus/rho4u mops-short spec.
    - But the addAtom `???` hole means coverage of THAT spec
      is partial, not complete.
    - The §7 vocabulary distinguishes "faithful" (alignment
      with the source spec, regardless of completeness) from
      "partial-coverage" (some spec features not implemented).
    - Per V2-PROTOCOL-1 specificity, "partial-coverage" is
      MORE specific because it captures both the alignment
      AND the incompleteness, whereas "faithful" only
      captures alignment.

  Codex's `[FORMALMETTA-PARTIAL-COVERAGE]` wins.
