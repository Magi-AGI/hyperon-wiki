Q2. MORK SPACE TYPE vs HYPERON SPACEMUT.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[INFERRED] A `MorkSpace` could implement `SpaceMut`, but the translation cost is high. MORK's `Expr` is a raw byte-pointer/buffer-based representation (space.rs:450), while Hyperon uses an `Atom` enum. Identity would need to be mapped from structural Hyperon atoms to MORK's path-based trie keys.

[VERIFIED] MORK's `static mut` counters are globally shared and not per-instance. Bridging to Hyperon's `SpaceObserver` would require a custom emit-on-write shim inside a wrapper.

VERDICT: [NOT-A-PHASE4-SUBSTRATE]. MORK is architecturally distinct and lacks the required observer surface.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q12. ARCHITECTURAL SYNTHESIS.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[RECOMMENDED PATH] Stay with **Hyperon Space** for Phase 4.

(a) Phase 4 Substrate: Hyperon Space. Alternate substrates lack required observability (SpaceObserver) and add significant adapter overhead.
(b) Phase 5+ Durability: **ROCKS-PATH** is the primary recommendation due to maturity and maintainer support. **MORK-PATH** is secondary for RAM-bound trie-native research.
(c) Patterns: No substrate patterns adopted for Phase 4. D3-1 metadata is sufficient.
(d) mork_ffi: Treat as reference/checkpoint, not production.
